Why "Thinking, Fast and Slow" Is My Favorite Non-Fiction Book Lately

Author: Daniel Kahneman

Review Excerpt:As someone who's deeply invested in technology, logic and decision making, I often find myself seeking out books that help me better understand how we, as humans, process information and make choices.

As someone who is passionate about technology and decision making I recently read Thinking Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman and it has become one of my favorite non fiction books. The book explains how we think in two main ways fast intuitive decisions called System 1 and slow logical thinking called System 2. While System 1 helps us make quick decisions it is also the source of many biases and errors. System 2 is more deliberate and requires effort which often leads us to default to System 1 when we should be thinking more carefully. Kahneman also discusses cognitive biases like the planning fallacy which explains why we often underestimate the time and resources needed for tasks. As a software engineer this insight has been invaluable in improving my approach to problem solving and project management. In a fast paced world it is easy to make quick decisions but the book reminds me of the power of slowing down and engaging in more reflective thinking. This shift in mindset has helped me make better choices both professionally and personally. Thinking Fast and Slow is an eye opening read that has helped me better understand my decision making process and become more mindful in the way I approach complex challenges.
